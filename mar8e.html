Streams in the Desert - March 8

"So now, O Lord, may the promise you made about your servant and his family become a permanent reality! Do as you promised, so it may become a reality and you may gain lasting fame, as people say, ‘The Lord who commands armies is the God of Israel.’ David’s dynasty will be established before you." (1 Chronicles 17:23-24)

This is a beautiful way to pray! 🙏 Many times, we ask for things without knowing if they are truly promised by God. So, we keep praying and waiting, unsure if it aligns with His plan. But sometimes, like in David’s case, we are absolutely sure that what we are asking for is within God’s will. ❤️

When we find a promise in the Bible that speaks directly to us, we should hold onto it and confidently say, “Do as You have said.” 📖 This is a strong and safe place to be—standing firmly on God’s Word. Instead of struggling or worrying, we can simply show the promise, like a check, and expect it to be fulfilled. ✅

It would make prayer much more exciting if we were more specific 🎯. It is better to claim a few promises clearly than to ask for many things vaguely. — F. B. Meyer

Every Bible promise is a message from God 📜, and we can bring it before Him with a simple request: “Do as You have said.” He will never deceive His children, and He will always keep His Word. 🤝

“Remember the word to Your servant, on which You have caused me to hope.” This is a powerful way to pray. 🛐 If God gave the promise, won’t He keep it? If He made us hope in it, will He disappoint us? — C. H. Spurgeon

“Being absolutely certain that whatever promise He is bound by, He is able also to make good.” (Romans 4:21, Weymouth’s Translation)

God is forever faithful! 🌟 Unlike human promises, which can be broken 💔, God’s promises are eternally trustworthy. 🕊️ Since the beginning of time, He has never failed a single one of His trusting children.

It is sad when a Christian hesitates at the door of a promise 🚪, especially in tough times 🌧️, afraid to enter. Instead, we should come boldly into our Father’s house 🏠 for shelter and comfort. — Gurnal

Every promise of God is built on four strong pillars: 🏛️
  • His justice and holiness ⚖️ – He will never deceive.
  • His grace and goodness ❤️ – He will never forget.
  • His truth 🕊️ – He will never change.
  • His power 💪 – He can fulfill what He has promised.
