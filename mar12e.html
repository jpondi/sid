🌪️ Streams in the Desert - March 12 🌪️

So Moses stretched out his staff over Egypt, and the Lord sent an east wind that blew all day and all night. By morning, the wind had brought locusts! Then the Lord sent a strong west wind, which lifted the locusts and blew them into the Red Sea. Not a single locust was left in Egypt. (Exodus 10:13,19)

In ancient times, when the Lord fought for Israel against cruel Pharaoh, stormy winds brought their deliverance. It seemed harsh—Israel was trapped between the wild sea, rocky cliffs, and a gathering storm. They cried, “The Egyptians are coming!” 🏞️🌊

But just when all hope seemed lost, the stormy wind pushed back the waves, and Israel marched through the sea on dry ground. 🌬️✨ The sea walls glowed with God’s glory, and the storm thundered above. By dawn, all of Israel had crossed safely. 🕊️

Then Israel sang a song of praise: “The stormy wind fulfills His word.” 🎶

“The enemy said, ‘I will chase, I will catch, I will take the plunder…’ But You blew with Your wind, and the sea covered them. They sank like lead in the mighty waters.”

One day, by God’s mercy, we will stand on the sea of glass 🎨, holding harps, and sing the song of Moses and the Lamb: “Your ways are just and true, King of saints.” We’ll see how the stormy winds brought our deliverance. 🌈

Now, you only see the mystery of sorrow. Later, you’ll see how the enemy was swept away in the storm. 🌪️ Now, you see only loss. Later, you’ll see how it broke the chains of evil. 🔗 Now, you fear the howling winds and thunder. Later, you’ll see how they cleared your path to the Promised Land. 🌄

“Though winds are wild,
And the gale unleashed,
My trusting heart still sings:
I know they mean no harm to me,
He rides on their wings.” 🕊️🌬️

—Mark Guy Pearse
