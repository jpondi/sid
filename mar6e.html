🌿 Always Trusting - Streams in the Desert - March 6 🌟

📖 “But we had hoped that he was the one who was going to redeem Israel. Not only this, but it is now the third day since these things happened” (Luke 24:21).

😔 I have always felt so sorry that on their walk to Emmaus, the disciples didn’t say to Jesus, “We still trust”. Instead, they said, “We trusted.” 💔 It’s so sad—it feels like their faith was completely gone. 😞

If only they had said, “Everything is against our hope; it looks like our trust is in vain, but we won’t give up. We believe we’ll see Him again.” 🙏✨ But no—they walked beside Him, talking about their lost faith. 😢 This made Him say, “O fools, and slow of heart to believe!” 💡

⚠️ Are we not in the same danger of hearing these words said to us? 🤔 Let’s remember: we can afford to lose anything and everything, but we can’t afford to lose our faith in the God of truth and love. ❤️🔥

💡 Let’s never put our faith in the past tense— “We trusted.” ❌ Instead, let us boldly say, “I am trusting.” ✅💪

🌈🕊️ A Gentle Reminder:

🌞 The soft, sweet summer was warm and glowing,
🌸 Bright were the blossoms on every bough:
🌹 I trusted Him when the roses were blooming;
💖 I trust Him now…

☁️ Small were my faith should it weakly falter
🍂 Now that the roses have ceased to blow;
🌧️ Frail were the trust that now should alter,
⚡ Doubting His love when storm clouds grow. ⛈️
—The Song of a Bird in a Winter Storm 🐦❄️

💖✨ Let’s keep trusting, no matter the season! 🌟🙌
