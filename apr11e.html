🌵 Streams in the Desert - April 11 🌵

"What I tell you in the darkness, speak ye in the light" (Matthew 10:27).

🙏 Jesus often brings us into dark places to share special truths with us. He takes us:

• 🌑 Into the shadow of grief when we lose someone dear

• 🚪 Into loneliness when sickness keeps us from normal life

• 💔 Into sadness when our dreams get broken

✨ In these dark places, He shares His wonderful secrets with us. Our eyes, blinded by life's bright lights, begin to see heavenly truths. Our ears, deafened by the world's noise, start to hear God's quiet voice.

⚖️ But these special messages come with responsibility - we must "speak in the light" and "proclaim from the rooftops."

🌤️ We're not meant to stay in the dark forever. When God calls us back to busy life, we must share what we've learned.

💡 This gives new meaning to hard times. Often the worst part of suffering is feeling useless: "I'm not helping anyone!" "Why is my life being wasted?"

🌄 But God has a purpose. He takes us to higher places to meet with Him face-to-face, so we can bring His message to others below.

⛰️ Was Moses' 40 days on the mountain wasted? Or Elijah's time at Horeb? Or Paul's years in Arabia?

🛐 There are no shortcuts to a faith-filled life. We need quiet times alone with God - moments on mountaintops, rest in valleys, nights under stars when the world is quiet and we can see eternal things clearly.

💞 Only then can we truly know God's constant presence and say with the Psalmist: "You are near, O God."

— F. B. Meyer

🌸 "Some hearts, like evening primroses, open more beautifully in the shadows of life." 🌸
