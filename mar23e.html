Streams in the Desert - March 24 🌿🌞

And Jacob said, "O God of my father Abraham, and God of my father Isaac, the Lord who said to me, 'Return to your country and to your family, and I will treat you well.' Deliver me, I pray." (Genesis 32:9, 11) 🙏✨

There are many good signs in this prayer. In some ways, it can be a model for us when we are going through hard times and our hearts are heavy with sorrow. 💔🌧️

Jacob started by reminding God of His promise: "You said." He did this twice (verses 9 and 12). Wow, Jacob was holding God to His word! 📖🤯 When we pray like this, God cannot say no. He has to keep His promises. If Jacob trusted God’s promise so much, how much more should we? 🤔💡

When you pray, make sure you stand firmly on God’s promises. This will give you the strength to push open the gates of heaven and receive what you ask for. 💪🚪✨

Jesus wants us to be specific in our prayers. He asks, "What do you want Me to do for you?" when we come to Him in times of trouble. Be clear and bold in your requests if you want clear answers. Vague prayers often seem to go unanswered. Be specific! Write your prayer like a check, and it will be cashed in heaven when you ask in Jesus' name. 💌✍️💸

Miss Havergal once said: "Every year, almost every day, I see more clearly how the rest, joy, and power of our Christian life depend on one thing: taking God at His word. Believe that He means exactly what He says, and accept His words without changing them." 🌟📜🙌

Bring Christ’s words, His promises, and His sacrifice—His blood—with you when you pray. When you do, not a single blessing from heaven will be denied to you. ✨🕊️💖

—Adam Clarke 📖🖋️
