🌵 Sit Still - Streams in the Desert - April 7 🌅

🙌 "Their strength is to sit still." (Isaiah 30:7) ✝️

To truly know God, we must learn to be still inside. 🤫 I remember when I first discovered this. There was a crisis in my life - my whole being was shaking with worry, and I felt I needed to act immediately. But I couldn't do anything, and the person who could help wouldn't move. 😔

I felt like I might explode from all the chaos inside me. Then suddenly, God's quiet voice spoke to my heart: "Be still, and know that I am God." 🕊️ Those words had power! I listened. I made my body completely still. I calmed my troubled heart. I looked up and waited. Then I truly knew it was God - even in this emergency when I felt so helpless. And I rested in Him. 😌

This was an experience I wouldn't trade for anything! 🌟 And from this stillness came the power to handle the situation, which was soon resolved successfully. That's when I truly learned that my strength comes from sitting still in God. 🙏

— Hannah Whitall Smith ✍️

There's a special kind of quiet waiting that isn't laziness. It's a living stillness that grows from trust. 😇 Nervous tension isn't trust - it's just worry packed tight. ✨

🌪️ Not in the wild storm's crashing sound,

🙏 From hurried prayers and restless plea,
🧘 Be still - hear what God says to thee.

💪 New strength grows when we pause our quest;
🎶 The sweetest songs of heaven's choir,
🤫 Are silent for half an hour entire.

😌 O rest, in complete soul-quietude,
🤐 Leave words, pause prayer and gratitude;
👐 Let your whole being, in His control,
😊 Learn the full meaning of His voice and soul.

⛑️ Not taking heaven by force so bold;
